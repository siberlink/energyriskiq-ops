Add a new section to the Daily Intelligence Report called:

🎯 Today's Actionable Takeaways

Purpose:
This section should translate the report’s risk signals into practical, easy-to-understand market implications. It should appear near the top of the report, immediately after the Global Risk Tone section and before the Index Movement Summary.

The section should be concise, decision-focused, and useful for users who only have 2–3 minutes to read the report.

Structure:
Create four subsections:

1. Oil
2. European Gas
3. LNG
4. Risk Management

Each asset section should include:
- A clear directional bias
- 2–3 short actionable bullet points
- The bias should be derived from the existing risk indices, market reaction data, top risk events, correlations, regime classification, and scenario probabilities.

Use simple bias labels:
- Bearish
- Neutral
- Bullish
- Neutral to Bullish
- Neutral to Bearish

Example output:

🎯 Today's Actionable Takeaways

Oil

Bias: Bearish

- Brent downside pressure remains dominant.
- Easing Middle East tensions reduce geopolitical premium.
- Watch Iraq OPEC quota discussions.

European Gas

Bias: Neutral to Bullish

- TTF diverges from declining risk indices.
- Winter grid concerns continue to support prices.
- Monitor EU gas storage and Ukraine power system risks.

LNG

Bias: Neutral

- Shipping normalization limits upside.
- Monitor Asian freight and shipping developments.
- LNG remains sensitive to TTF moves and regional demand signals.

Risk Management

- Current regime remains Calm.
- No immediate need for aggressive hedging.
- Keep monitoring geopolitical escalation risk and storage-related stress.

Logic guidance:

Oil bias should become more bearish when:
- GERI is falling
- Middle East risk is easing
- Brent is already declining
- De-escalation probability is high
- Hormuz shipping flows normalize

Oil bias should become more bullish when:
- GERI is rising
- Middle East escalation probability increases
- Top risk events mention Hormuz, OPEC disruption, sanctions, war, or supply disruption
- Brent rises while risk indices rise

European Gas bias should become more bullish when:
- TTF rises despite declining global risk
- EU storage is moderate or low
- Europe-related events mention Ukraine grid risk, winter demand, supply stress, LNG constraints, or cold weather
- EGSI-M or EERI rises

European Gas bias should become more bearish when:
- TTF falls
- EU storage improves
- EERI and EGSI-M decline
- no Europe-specific risk events are present

LNG bias should become more bullish when:
- shipping disruption risk increases
- Asian demand or freight stress rises
- TTF rises strongly
- Middle East or maritime risk increases

LNG bias should become more bearish or neutral when:
- shipping flows normalize
- tanker rates fall
- global supply risk eases
- demand indicators weaken

Risk Management guidance:
- If regime is Calm and de-escalation probability is high, say aggressive hedging is not needed.
- If regime is Stabilizing, recommend selective hedging only.
- If regime is Elevated, recommend maintaining protection against price spikes.
- If regime is Crisis, recommend defensive hedging and close monitoring of exposed assets.

Tone:
Use professional but simple language.
Avoid long paragraphs.
Avoid generic text.
Each bullet should explain what the user should watch, reduce, maintain, or be aware of.

Important:
Do not invent unrelated assets.
Use only data already available in the Daily Intelligence Report.
Keep the section short enough to fit above the fold.
