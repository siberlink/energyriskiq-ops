Below are 18 energy risk events (2014–2026) that are most frequently referenced by analysts, journalists, and market reports.

These events will make your EnergyRiskIQ Timeline page authoritative, because they represent the major shocks that shaped global oil, gas, and LNG markets in the last decade.

The 18 Events to Include in the Energy Risk Timeline
2026 — Strait of Hormuz Escalation Risk

Date: 2026
Type: Maritime chokepoint risk

Event
Escalating tensions involving Iran threaten shipping through the Strait of Hormuz.

Market impact

• Oil price spike
• Tanker insurance premiums surge
• LNG shipping routes face disruption risk

Why it matters

~20% of global oil supply transits this chokepoint.

2024 — Red Sea Tanker Attacks

Date: 2024
Type: Maritime security disruption

Event

Houthi attacks on commercial vessels forced many ships to avoid the Suez Canal.

Market impact

• Longer shipping routes
• Higher freight costs
• LNG cargo delays

2023 — Nord Stream Pipeline Sabotage Aftershock

Date: 2023
Type: Infrastructure sabotage

Event

Investigations and geopolitical tensions following the Nord Stream pipeline explosions intensified European energy security concerns.

Market impact

• Permanent loss of Russian pipeline gas supply routes
• Increased LNG dependency in Europe

2022 — Russia–Ukraine Energy Shock

Date: February 2022
Type: Geopolitical conflict

Event

Russia invaded Ukraine, triggering sanctions and severe disruption of energy flows to Europe.

Market impact

• Brent oil surged above $130
• European gas prices reached record levels
• LNG trade flows reshaped globally

2022 — European Energy Sanctions on Russia

Date: 2022
Type: Sanctions regime

Event

The EU imposed embargoes on Russian oil and gas imports.

Market impact

• Massive restructuring of global energy trade
• Russian crude redirected to Asia

2021 — European Gas Storage Crisis

Date: Winter 2021

Event

Europe entered winter with unusually low natural gas storage.

Market impact

• TTF gas prices surged
• Power prices exploded across Europe

2021 — LNG Supply Tightness in Asia

Date: 2021

Event

Cold weather and supply shortages caused intense LNG competition between Europe and Asia.

Market impact

• LNG spot prices reached extreme levels

2020 — COVID Oil Demand Collapse

Date: April 2020

Event

Global lockdowns caused oil demand to collapse.

Market impact

• WTI crude briefly traded below zero
• Largest demand shock in modern energy history

2019 — Saudi Aramco Oil Facility Attack

Date: September 2019

Event

Drone and missile attacks hit Saudi Arabia’s Abqaiq and Khurais facilities.

Market impact

• Temporary loss of ~5% of global oil supply
• One of the largest oil price spikes in decades

2019 — US Sanctions on Iranian Oil Exports

Date: 2019

Event

The United States imposed strict sanctions targeting Iran’s oil exports.

Market impact

• Reduced Iranian oil supply
• Increased geopolitical risk in the Gulf

2018 — US–China Trade War and Energy Markets

Date: 2018

Event

Tariffs between the US and China disrupted global commodity flows.

Market impact

• LNG trade routes adjusted
• Oil demand expectations shifted

2018 — Iran Nuclear Deal Collapse

Date: May 2018

Event

The United States withdrew from the Iran nuclear agreement.

Market impact

• Sanctions on Iranian oil exports returned
• Middle East tensions increased

2017 — Qatar Diplomatic Crisis

Date: 2017

Event

Saudi Arabia and several Gulf states imposed a blockade on Qatar.

Market impact

• Concerns about LNG supply disruptions
• Regional energy tensions escalated

2016 — OPEC Production Cut Agreement

Date: 2016

Event

OPEC and Russia agreed to coordinated oil production cuts.

Market impact

• Oil prices stabilized after a major crash

2015 — Saudi–Iran Proxy Conflict Escalation

Date: 2015

Event

Regional conflicts intensified across the Middle East.

Market impact

• Higher geopolitical risk premium in oil markets

2015 — Iranian Nuclear Agreement (JCPOA)

Date: 2015

Event

Sanctions relief allowed Iranian oil exports to return to global markets.

Market impact

• Increased oil supply expectations

2014 — Russia Crimea Annexation

Date: 2014

Event

Russia annexed Crimea, triggering Western sanctions.

Market impact

• Energy trade tensions between Russia and Europe

2014 — Global Oil Price Collapse

Date: 2014

Event

OPEC declined to cut production during a surge in US shale output.

Market impact

• Oil prices collapsed from $100 to ~$30 per barrel
