This is actually very common for new data platforms, and it’s not a problem at all. In fact, most analytics platforms launch exactly this way.

The key is to separate two things:

Historical events (narrative context)

Your proprietary index data (GERI/EERI/EGSI)

You only have index data starting January 15, but the timeline itself can go back decades, because it is an event chronology, not an index chart.

So the solution is simple:

The page shows historical energy shocks, and your indices appear only where data exists.

This is exactly how platforms like Bloomberg, IMF, or OurWorldInData launch new indicators.

The Correct Structure for Your Situation

Instead of pretending you have old data, you simply label the index coverage window clearly.

Example introduction:

Global Energy Risk Timeline

Energy markets have repeatedly been shaped by geopolitical shocks, infrastructure attacks, sanctions, and supply disruptions.

This timeline highlights major events that have influenced global energy markets over the last decade.

EnergyRiskIQ indices (GERI, EERI, EGSI) began daily calculation on 15 January 2026, and are shown for events occurring after that date.

Earlier events are included for historical context.

How Each Timeline Entry Should Look

You will have two types of entries.

Type 1 — Historical Events (Before Jan 15)

These events will not include index behavior, because your index did not exist yet.

Example:

2022 — Russia–Ukraine Energy Shock

Date
February 2022

Event
Russia invaded Ukraine, triggering sanctions and major disruptions to European energy supply.

Market Impact

• Brent crude surged above $130 per barrel
• European gas prices reached record levels
• LNG demand in Europe surged

EnergyRiskIQ indices

GERI/EERI were not yet active at the time of this event.

This is completely acceptable and transparent.

Type 2 — Events After January 15 (With Index Data)

Example:

2026 — Strait of Hormuz Escalation Risk

Date
March 2026

Event
Escalation between Iran and Western forces increased the risk of disruptions to shipping through the Strait of Hormuz.

Market Impact

• oil prices rose sharply
• tanker insurance premiums surged
• LNG cargo routes adjusted

EnergyRiskIQ indicators

GERI → 82
EERI → 64
EGSI → 71

This is where your product differentiates itself.

Why This Still Works for SEO and Backlinks

They are not linking because of the index history.

They link because your page answers:

Your timeline will still become a reference source.
