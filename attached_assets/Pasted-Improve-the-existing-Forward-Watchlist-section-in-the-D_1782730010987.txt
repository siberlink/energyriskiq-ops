Improve the existing Forward Watchlist section in the Daily Intelligence Report.

Purpose:
The Forward Watchlist should tell users what to monitor over the next 24 hours based on the report’s current risk signals, market moves, events, and scenario outlook.

Goal:
Never show an empty or generic message such as “No high-priority items.”
The section should always provide 3–5 relevant watchlist items.

Placement:
Keep this section in the existing Forward Watchlist area, preferably after Top Risk Events and before Scenario Forecasts.

Section title:
Forward Watchlist — Next 24h

Structure:
Each watchlist item should include:
- A short title
- A priority level
- A one-sentence reason why it matters

Priority levels:
- High
- Medium
- Low

Example output:

Forward Watchlist — Next 24h

1. Iraq OPEC quota discussions
Priority: High
Why it matters: Any change in production policy could affect Brent risk premium and short-term oil positioning.

2. Strait of Hormuz shipping flows
Priority: Medium
Why it matters: Normalizing flows would reduce geopolitical premium, while renewed disruption would increase upside risk for oil and LNG.

3. Ukraine winter power system risks
Priority: Medium
Why it matters: Grid stress can increase European gas sensitivity and support TTF during colder demand periods.

4. EU gas storage injections
Priority: Medium
Why it matters: Storage trends influence winter supply confidence and European gas price risk.

5. Asian LNG freight developments
Priority: Low
Why it matters: Freight or shipping stress can affect LNG pricing and regional supply flexibility.

Logic guidance:

If Middle East, Hormuz, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Red Sea, maritime, or sanctions events are present:
Include at least one oil or shipping-related watchlist item.

If Brent is moving strongly:
Include a Brent-specific watchlist item explaining whether the move confirms or contradicts the risk indices.

If GERI is rising:
Prioritize geopolitical escalation, oil supply disruption, maritime security, and Brent risk premium.

If GERI is falling:
Prioritize confirmation signals such as shipping normalization, lower volatility, Brent downside follow-through, and de-escalation durability.

If EERI or EGSI-M is rising:
Include European gas, TTF, storage, LNG imports, Ukraine grid, winter demand, or pipeline flow risks.

If TTF is rising while GERI is falling:
Include a divergence item explaining that European gas fundamentals may be outweighing global risk easing.

If LNG risk is elevated:
Include Asian LNG demand, freight rates, shipping disruption, Panama/Suez/Red Sea route risk, or TTF-to-LNG spillover.

If the risk regime is Calm:
Items should focus on confirmation and early warning signals, not crisis language.

If the risk regime is Elevated:
Items should focus on escalation triggers, hedging signals, and assets most exposed to price spikes.

If the risk regime is Crisis:
Items should focus on immediate disruption indicators, emergency policy actions, shipping flows, and supply availability.

Fallback logic:
If there are no strong events, generate a conservative watchlist based on:
- Brent trend confirmation
- TTF trend confirmation
- EU gas storage movement
- GERI direction
- LNG freight or shipping conditions

Tone:
Professional, concise, practical, and forward-looking.
Avoid generic language.
Avoid repeating the same watchlist every day unless the same risk is still active.
Each item should help the user know what to monitor next.

Important:
Use only data already available in the Daily Intelligence Report.
Do not invent unrelated events.
Do not create investment advice.
Do not exceed 5 watchlist items.
Do not leave the section empty.
