Build a feature that automatically generates a LinkedIn post every day before 08:00 based on the latest “Daily Intelligence Report”.

Goal:
Create a daily LinkedIn-ready post for EnergyRiskIQ using the existing Daily Intelligence Report data. The post should follow a consistent structure, but the hook, angle, wording, and CTA should rotate daily so the content does not feel repetitive.

Core output:
Generate one LinkedIn post per day with this structure:

EnergyRiskIQ Daily Intelligence | [Current Date]

[Rotating strong hook]

[Analysis and insights]

Today's key takeaways:

• [Takeaway 1]
• [Takeaway 2]
• [Takeaway 3]
• [Optional Takeaway 4]

[CTA / Question]

#EnergyMarkets #EnergyRisk #OilMarkets #NaturalGas #LNG #CommodityTrading #RiskManagement #EnergyRiskIQ

Data source:
Use the latest generated Daily Intelligence Report as the source of truth.

Extract and summarize from these report sections:

* Executive One-Line Summary
* Today's Actionable Takeaways
* Brent Outlook
* Natural Gas Outlook
* LNG Outlook
* Forward Watchlist
* Scenario Forecasts
* Key Geopolitical Developments
* Energy Market Implications

Logic:

1. Fetch the latest Daily Intelligence Report generated for the current day.
2. Identify the dominant market theme:

   * Geopolitical risk
   * Oil / Brent
   * Natural gas
   * LNG
   * European gas storage
   * Supply disruption
   * Market volatility
   * Risk premium fading
   * Risk underpricing
3. Select a hook category based on the dominant theme.
4. Rotate hooks so the same hook is not repeated within the last 14 days.
5. Generate 2–4 short insight paragraphs explaining:

   * What changed
   * Why it matters
   * What markets may be underpricing
   * What energy professionals should monitor next
6. Generate 3–4 key takeaways from the report.
7. Select a CTA question based on the dominant theme.
8. Keep the tone professional, analytical, clear, and LinkedIn-native.
9. Avoid sounding promotional or exaggerated.
10. Do not invent data. Only use data available in the report.
11. If no fresh report exists, do not generate a post. Show a warning: “No Daily Intelligence Report available for today.”

Hook rotation examples:

Geopolitical risk:

* Markets may be underpricing geopolitical risk.
* Risk often moves before headlines.
* Energy markets rarely wait for tomorrow's headlines.
* Political risk can move faster than prices.

Oil / Brent:

* Brent's risk premium is shifting again.
* Oil markets are calm, but risk signals are still moving.
* Brent prices do not always reflect the full risk picture.
* The oil market may be pricing today, not tomorrow.

Natural gas:

* European gas storage is improving, but vulnerabilities remain.
* Gas market comfort can disappear quickly.
* Storage levels alone do not guarantee winter security.
* Natural gas risk is rarely visible in one headline number.

LNG:

* LNG markets can change direction faster than most expect.
* LNG prices can remain stable right until they suddenly don't.
* One LNG disruption can reshape regional pricing quickly.
* LNG risk is often global before it becomes local.

Contrarian:

* The biggest energy market risks are often invisible in today's prices.
* Calm prices do not always mean low risk.
* Stability can be misleading in energy markets.
* The market may be quiet, but the risk picture is not.

Volatility:

* Volatility often begins long before prices react.
* Market stress usually builds before it becomes visible.
* Risk indicators can move before volatility appears.
* Low volatility does not always mean low uncertainty.

CTA question rotation examples:

* What risk do you believe energy markets are currently underpricing?
* Which energy risk are you watching most closely today?
* Do current prices fully reflect the risk picture?
* Which indicator would you monitor first this week?
* What could trigger the next major move in energy markets?
* Are markets too comfortable with current supply conditions?

Requirements:

* Create a stored table or collection for generated LinkedIn posts.
* Store: date, report_id, selected_theme, selected_hook, post_body, status, created_at.
* Status options: draft, approved, published.
* The system should generate the post as draft only. Do not auto-publish.
* Add a simple admin UI section where I can view, copy, edit, approve, or delete each generated post.
* Add a button: “Generate Today’s LinkedIn Post”.
* Add another button: “Regenerate with different hook”.
* Add another button: “Copy LinkedIn Post”.
* Add validation to keep the post concise and readable.
* Target length: 1,200–1,800 characters.
* Avoid more than 8 hashtags.
* Keep paragraphs short.
* Do not use emojis except bullet points.
* Do not use hype language such as “game-changing”, “explosive”, “must-read”, or “shocking”.

Scheduling:
Automatically generate the draft every day before 08:00 Europe/Amsterdam time, ideally between 07:00 and 07:30, after the Daily Intelligence Report has been created.

Fallback logic:
If the Daily Intelligence Report is not available by 07:30, retry every 10 minutes until 08:00.
If still unavailable, do not generate the post and show an error in the admin UI.
