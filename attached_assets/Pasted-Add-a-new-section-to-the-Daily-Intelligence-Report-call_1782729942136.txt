Add a new section to the Daily Intelligence Report called:

Executive One-Line Summary

Purpose:
This section should give the user the most important conclusion from the entire report in one clear sentence.

Placement:
Show this immediately below the Global Risk Tone section and above the “Today's Actionable Takeaways” section.

Goal:
A user should understand the daily market message in less than 10 seconds.

Content rules:
- Generate only one sentence.
- Keep it between 18 and 30 words.
- Make it practical, not generic.
- Mention the overall risk direction.
- Mention the main market implication.
- Mention at least one key affected market when possible: Brent, TTF, LNG, European gas, oil, or broader energy markets.
- Avoid vague phrases like “markets remain uncertain” unless supported by the data.
- Do not repeat the exact same wording every day.

Example output:

Executive One-Line Summary

Global energy risk continues to ease, reducing geopolitical support for Brent while leaving European gas exposed to winter-related stress.

Logic guidance:

If GERI is falling and de-escalation probability is high:
Summarize that global risk is easing and oil risk premium is declining.

Example:
Global energy risk is easing, reducing upside pressure on Brent while keeping attention on European gas and winter supply risks.

If GERI is rising and escalation probability is high:
Summarize that global risk is increasing and energy markets face stronger upside risk.

Example:
Global energy risk is rising, increasing upside pressure on Brent and LNG as supply disruption risks return to focus.

If EERI or EGSI-M is rising while GERI is stable or falling:
Summarize that European energy stress is diverging from the global risk picture.

Example:
Global risk remains stable, but European gas stress is increasing as TTF and winter supply risks diverge from broader market calm.

If Brent is falling while risk indices are falling:
Summarize that oil downside pressure remains dominant due to easing geopolitical premium.

Example:
Easing geopolitical risk continues to weigh on Brent, with oil markets pricing out part of the recent risk premium.

If TTF is rising while risk indices are falling:
Summarize the divergence.

Example:
TTF strength is diverging from easing global risk, suggesting European gas fundamentals are currently more important than geopolitics.

If LNG risk is elevated:
Summarize shipping, Asian demand, or maritime disruption risks.

Example:
LNG risk remains elevated as shipping and Asian demand signals keep the market sensitive despite a calmer global risk backdrop.

If the regime is Calm:
Use language such as:
- risk remains contained
- no immediate escalation signal
- markets are stabilizing

If the regime is Elevated:
Use language such as:
- risk pressure is building
- markets face renewed upside exposure
- hedging attention should increase

If the regime is Crisis:
Use language such as:
- acute disruption risk
- defensive positioning is justified
- high-impact energy market moves remain possible

Tone:
Professional, concise, direct, and useful for energy traders, analysts, risk managers, and procurement teams.

Important:
Use only the data already available in the Daily Intelligence Report.
Do not invent market events.
Do not generate multiple summaries.
Do not make investment advice claims.
The sentence should sound like a professional morning briefing headline.
