Improvements That Would Make It Even Better

These are small tweaks, not redesigns.

1. Add One Title Above the Map

Right now the title is good but could be stronger for SEO.

Suggested title:

Global Energy Crisis Map — Major Risk Hotspots & Energy Chokepoints

This adds keywords:

• energy chokepoints
• energy crisis map

2. Add Year Markers to Crisis Regions

Right now the map shows regions but not time reference.

Example improvement:

Russia–Ukraine
2014 / 2022
Red Sea
2024
Saudi Aramco
2019

Journalists love date anchors.

3. Slightly Increase the Visibility of Hormuz

Hormuz is arguably the most important energy chokepoint on Earth.

You may want to slightly emphasize it.

Example:

Increase the circle size slightly.

This visually reinforces:

20% of global oil flows

4. Add a Small Annotation

Example:

Circle radius reflects relative severity of recorded disruptions.

This helps interpretation.

5. Add a Source Line

Very important for credibility.

Add at bottom:

Sources: IEA, EIA, Reuters, EnergyRiskIQ analysis

Journalists trust maps more when sources are explicit.

6. Add One More Region (Optional but Powerful)

Consider adding:

South China Sea

Reason:

• major shipping corridor
• LNG routes
• China–Taiwan geopolitical tension

Even if no event yet, analysts expect this to be a future hotspot.

7. Allow Image Download

I noticed the Download Map button.

Excellent.

But make sure it exports:

• PNG
• high resolution
• white background version (optional)

Journalists often embed these images.

One Very Smart SEO Trick

Below the map add:

Why Energy Crises Cluster Around These Regions

Short explanation:

Most global energy disruptions originate from a small number of geographic chokepoints where energy supply, shipping routes, and geopolitical tensions intersect.

This creates indexable text for Google.

The Strategic Value of This Map

This single map can generate a large number of backlinks.

Journalists writing about:

• oil supply risk
• Strait of Hormuz
• Red Sea crisis
• Russian gas

will often search:

“global energy chokepoints map”

Your page could rank for this.
