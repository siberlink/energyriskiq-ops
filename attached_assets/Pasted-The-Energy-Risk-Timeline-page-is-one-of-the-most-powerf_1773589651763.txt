1. Purpose of the Energy Risk Timeline

Core idea

Create a chronological map of major energy shocks affecting global oil, gas, and energy infrastructure.

Energy markets experience repeated geopolitical disruptions — wars, sanctions, infrastructure sabotage, maritime chokepoint risks, cyberattacks, etc.

Examples include:

Russia–Ukraine war disrupting European energy supply

Middle East conflicts threatening shipping routes

LNG infrastructure disruptions

Pipeline sabotage

Maritime chokepoint closures

For example, the Russia–Ukraine invasion in 2022 pushed Brent crude prices to around $139 per barrel, the highest since 2008.

Similarly, disruption risks around the Strait of Hormuz threaten 20% of global oil shipments, making it one of the most critical energy chokepoints.

Your page would visually demonstrate how energy risk repeatedly shocks markets over time.
